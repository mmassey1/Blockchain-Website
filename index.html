Let's Learn About Blockchain!

You may be wondering, what even is a blockchain anyway?

Well, first let me explain what a block is.

What is a 'block?'

  • In terms of cryptology, a block is defined as a place in blockchain where information is stored and encrypted.
  • Blocks are identified by long numbers that include encrypted transaction information from previous blocks and new transaction information.
  • Blocks are important in database management.

Each block has 3 main elements, which are:

  1. Data: The type of data depends on what the blockchain is being used for. In Bitcoin, for example, a block’s data contains the details about the transaction including sender, receiver, number of coins, and so on.
  2. Hash: A hash in blockchain is something like a fingerprint or signature. It identifies a block and all its content, and it’s always unique.
  3. Hash of previous block: This piece is precisely what makes a blockchain! Because each block carries the information of the previous block, the chain becomes very secure.

To put it simply, a block stores information. It can store many pieces of information while occupying small amounts of space.
When used in cryptocurrency, a block maintains a record of transactions, which helps the system keep track of what happens during a transaction.

What is 'blockchain'?

Blockchain is a system of recording information in a way that makes it difficult or impossible to change, hack, or cheat the system.

Remember...

Blockchain stores information in batches called blocks. These blocks are linked together in a sequential way to form a continuous line. A chain of blocks. A blockchain. Each block is like a page of a ledger or a record book.
