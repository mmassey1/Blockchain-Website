The Uses & Limitations of Blockchain

Now that I've properly introduced the concept of a blockchain, let's talk about how people use it!

Since its introduction, blockchain has been adopted for a wide range of uses even beyond just cryptocurrencies. It has the potential to revolutionize many different industries by providing a secure and transparent way to track and verify transactions. Some potential uses for blockchain include

  • Financial transactions: Blockchain can be used to facilitate secure and transparent financial transactions, such as the transfer of money or the trading of assets.
  • Supply chain management: Blockchain can be used to track the movement of goods through the supply chain, providing greater transparency and accountability.
  • Identity verification: Blockchain can be used to securely store and verify identity information, making it easier to confirm the identity of individuals or organizations.
  • Voting systems: Blockchain can be used to create secure and transparent voting systems, which could help to improve the integrity and trustworthiness of elections.
  • Medical record keeping: Blockchain can be used to store and manage medical records, providing a secure and accessible way to track and share patient information.
  • Art and collectibles: Blockchain can be used to authenticate and track the ownership of art, antiques, and other collectibles, helping to combat fraud and improve the security of the market.
However, the 2 most popular uses for blockchain currently are cryptocurrency and NFTs.

Limitations of Blockchain

While the potential uses for blockchain are vast, it is still a relatively new technology and there are many challenges to its widespread adoption. These challenges include issues related to scalability, regulation, and the need for greater technological infrastructure. Despite these challenges, the use of blockchain is growing and it is expected to have a significant impact on many industries in the coming years.
